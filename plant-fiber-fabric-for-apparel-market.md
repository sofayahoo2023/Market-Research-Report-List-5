<p><p>Plant fiber fabrics for apparel are generally categorized into three main types: seed fiber, bast fiber, and leaf fiber. Seed fiber is derived from the seeds of plants like cotton and kapok, while bast fiber comes from the stems of plants like flax and hemp. Leaf fiber, on the other hand, is obtained from the leaves of plants like pineapple and banana. Each type of plant fiber fabric has unique properties and applications, catering to the diverse needs of the apparel market.</p></p>
