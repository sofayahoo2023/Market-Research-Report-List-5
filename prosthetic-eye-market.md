<p><p>Non-integrated implants in the prosthetic eye market are removable and are not directly attached to the eye socket, allowing for easy cleaning and maintenance. Integrated implants, on the other hand, are surgically attached to the eye socket, providing a more natural appearance and greater stability. While non-integrated implants are less invasive and can be easily replaced, integrated implants offer a more permanent solution and can help improve facial symmetry and self-esteem for the wearer. Both types cater to different needs and preferences within the prosthetic eye market.</p></p>
