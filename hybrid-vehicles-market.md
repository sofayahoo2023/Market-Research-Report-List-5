<p><p>Hybrid vehicles come in three main types: series hybrid, parallel hybrid, and series-parallel hybrid. In a series hybrid, the internal combustion engine is used solely to generate electricity to power an electric motor, while in a parallel hybrid, both the engine and electric motor can power the vehicle independently or simultaneously. A series-parallel hybrid combines aspects of both configurations, allowing for more flexibility in how the vehicle is powered. Each type offers different benefits in terms of fuel efficiency and performance.</p></p>
