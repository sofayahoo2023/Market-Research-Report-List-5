<p><p>Portable gas chromatography is a method used to separate and analyze compounds in a mixture. Gas-solid chromatography (GSC) involves the interaction between the sample and a solid stationary phase, while gas-liquid chromatography (GLC) uses a liquid stationary phase. GSC is ideal for analyzing low molecular weight compounds, while GLC is suitable for analyzing volatile and thermally stable compounds. Both GSC and GLC are widely used in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, environmental monitoring, and food and beverage for their accuracy and efficiency.</p></p>
