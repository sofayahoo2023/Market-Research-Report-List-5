<p><p>Evolved Gas Analysis (EGA) is a technique used to analyze gases released from a sample as it undergoes thermal decomposition or other processes. There are several market types for EGA, including FTIR, QMS, GCMS, and ELIF. Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR) analyzes gases based on their absorption of infrared radiation. Quadrupole mass spectrometry (QMS) measures the mass-to-charge ratio of ions in a sample. Gas chromatography mass spectrometry (GCMS) separates and identifies gases based on their retention times. Enhanced laser-induced fluorescence (ELIF) uses laser excitation to analyze gas samples.</p></p>
