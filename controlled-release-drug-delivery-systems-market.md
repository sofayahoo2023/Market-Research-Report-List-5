<p><p>Controlled-release drug delivery systems refer to mechanisms that enable timed and targeted release of a drug into the body. Various market types include Wurster Technique, Coacervation, Micro Encapsulation, Implants, Transdermal, Targeted Delivery, and Others. Wurster Technique involves coating particles with a semipermeable membrane. Coacervation is a phase separation process. Microencapsulation entails enclosing drug particles in polymer shells. Implants involve inserting drug-dispensing devices under the skin. Transdermal delivery administers drugs through the skin. Targeted delivery systems aim drugs at specific sites in the body for enhanced efficacy. Others include novel drug delivery technologies.</p></p>
