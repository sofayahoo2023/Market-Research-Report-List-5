Mechanical tests involve instruments that use mechanical force to measure clotting properties. Electrochemical tests use electrodes to detect changes in blood coagulation. Optical tests rely on light absorption or scattering to analyze clot formation. Other technologies may include molecular or genetic testing methods to assess coagulation factors. These different technologies offer a wide range of options for healthcare providers to diagnose and monitor coagulation disorders.</p></p>
