<p><p>Oral thin film drugs are a growing market with two main types: sublingual film and fully dissolving dental/buccal film. Sublingual films are placed under the tongue where the drug is absorbed through the oral mucosa. Fully dissolving dental/buccal films are applied to the teeth or inner cheek where they quickly dissolve and release the drug for absorption. Both types offer advantages such as ease of administration, rapid onset of action, and improved patient compliance compared to traditional oral dosage forms.</p></p>
