<p><p>Organic coagulants are substances used in the process of water treatment to remove impurities and particles by causing them to clump together and settle. The main types of organic coagulants in the market include Aluminum Sulfate, Polyaluminum Chloride, Ferric Chloride, and Ferrous Sulfate. These coagulants work by destabilizing the particles in water, allowing them to bind together and form larger flocs that can be easily removed. Other types of organic coagulants may include products like chitosan or polyacrylamide, which offer alternative methods of coagulation in water treatment processes.</p></p>
