<p><p>Blood pressure sensors are categorized based on the range of pressure they can measure. Sensors capable of measuring pressures from -30 to 100 mmHg are used for monitoring low blood pressure conditions. Those that can measure pressures from 100 to 300 mmHg are typically used for normal blood pressure monitoring. Sensors capable of measuring pressures above 300 mmHg are used for monitoring high blood pressure or hypertension. Each type of sensor caters to different medical conditions and plays a vital role in monitoring and managing blood pressure levels.</p></p>
