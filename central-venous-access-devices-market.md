<p><p>Central Venous Access Devices are used to administer medications, fluids, and nutrients directly into the bloodstream. The market types include Peripheral Venous Catheters, which are inserted into the veins of the arms or legs; Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC), which are inserted into larger veins for long-term use; Midline Catheters, which are placed in the upper arm veins for short-term use; and other devices such as tunneled catheters and implantable ports. Each type of device offers different levels of invasiveness and duration of use.</p></p>
