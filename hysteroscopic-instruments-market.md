This technology is especially useful in detecting abnormalities such as polyps, fibroids, or adhesions. On the other hand, microhysteroscopy is a minimally invasive hysteroscopic procedure that uses smaller instruments to perform diagnostic and therapeutic interventions with greater precision and minimal discomfort for the patient. Both types of hysteroscopy instruments have revolutionized the field of gynecology by improving diagnostic accuracy and patient outcomes.</p></p>
