<p><p>Handheld airborne particle counters can be classified based on whether they use ionizing or non-ionizing technology. Ionizing particle counters use a radioactive source to charge particles, while non-ionizing counters use lasers or light scattering to detect particles. Ionizing counters are typically more sensitive and accurate for detecting smaller particles, while non-ionizing counters are often preferred for their safety and ease of use. Both types of particle counters are used in various industries for monitoring air quality and identifying potential health hazards.</p></p>
