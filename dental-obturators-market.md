<p><ul><li>Definitive Obturators</li><li>Interim Obturators</li><li>Surgical Obturator</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dental obturators are custom-made devices used to close defects or openings in the oral cavity. Definitive obturators are permanent solutions for patients with congenital defects or surgical resections. Interim obturators are temporary devices used during the healing process or while a definitive obturator is being made. Surgical obturators are designed to aid in post-operative healing after surgical procedures. Other types of obturators may include those used for speech or swallowing difficulties. Each type serves a specific purpose in improving oral function and quality of life for patients.</p></p>
