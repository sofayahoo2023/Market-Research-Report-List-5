<p><p>Voice prostheses devices are used to help patients who have undergone a laryngectomy regain their ability to speak. There are two main types of voice prosthesis devices: indwelling and non-indwelling. Indwelling prostheses are permanently implanted in the patient's tracheoesophageal puncture and require regular replacement. Non-indwelling prostheses are inserted and removed as needed by the patient or healthcare provider. Both types of devices play a crucial role in restoring communication ability for individuals who have lost their voice due to surgery.</p></p>
