<p><p>FPGA Mezzanine Card (FMC) comes in two market types - Low Pin Count (LPC) and High Pin Count (HPC). LPC FMCs have fewer pins and are typically used in applications where space and cost are constraints. On the other hand, HPC FMCs have a higher pin count, enabling them to support more I/O options and higher bandwidth applications. Both LPC and HPC FMCs are widely used in the industry for interfacing FPGAs with external devices and systems, providing flexibility and scalability in FPGA-based designs.</p></p>
